Hi. I'm Kade.

I think the internet is dead, or at least dying. Or, in a sense, it's returning to whence it came - a system for linking machines. 

As I sit here typing this inside of an IDE, the built-in tab completion powered by AI is constantly racing me to determine what I think and what I should be typing next. Algorithms distilling the internet to predict the next token, the next sentence, the next intent of what I should be saying based on a high dimensional "neural" representation of my intent. 

It's an odd feeling to have your thoughts mediated by a machine, even if that machine is simply trying to help you (or is it?).

Having gone to military school for undergrad, the struggle for identity inside of a system trying to cast you into a specific mold, even if a good one, is something I'm intimately familiar with. In my time on the banks of the Severn, I at least had a notion of the objective function at play, what was rewarded and what was penalized. Here, it's less clear. There isn't a person steering the ship we find ourselves on, but rather a collection of incentives and algorithms that have been trained on the sum total of human expression, and are now using that to shape our future.

Every time the autocomplete suggests a sentence, it's a reminder that the space of possible things to say is shrinking. The odd emotional reaction to seeing the sentence you were about to type (or think you wanted to type) appear as if out of thin air at first was convenient but has since become unsettling. 

A weird feeling of prescience - the ability to see the future, but only in the sense that the future is being written for you, and you're just along for the ride. Just hit tab, and you're teleported ahead a glimpse in time to a potential future that you can either accept or reject. The collapse of the probability distribution of your free will into a sampled future. The strong desire to reject the ~~_suggestion, to assert your agency, is often met with the realization that the suggestion was, in fact, the more logical and coherent choice._~~ notion that an immense sequence model can accurately predict the sequence of events that is your life.
